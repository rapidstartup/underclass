This article presents a hypothetical scenario of an AI-driven economic crisis unfolding from late 2025 to 2028, framed as a "thought exercise" written in February 2026 but narrated from June 2028. It extrapolates from current (as of early 2026) AI trends, economic data, and tools to project negative feedback loops like mass white-collar job losses, wage collapses, and disruptions in sectors like software, intermediation, and consumer services. Below, I list the main hypotheticals, the real-world "today" items (facts, trends, or tools from 2025-early 2026) they project forward from, and sources verifying those real items. Sources are cited inline but the links follow at the bottom.
1. Hypothetical: Unemployment rises to 10.2% by June 2028, with the S&P 500 down 38% from October 2026 highs, driven by AI-induced white-collar layoffs starting in early 2026.
Real Basis: Initial AI-driven layoffs in 2026 are projected to spiral, based on current trends of AI boosting corporate margins through headcount reductions in tech and white-collar sectors. This draws from ongoing AI adoption in 2025-2026, where tools enable efficiency gains but begin displacing jobs.
Sources: ServiceNow's Q4 2025 financials show strong growth (21% YoY subscription revenue), but the article projects reflexive loops from such efficiencies; real 2025 layoffs in tech (e.g., AI replacing routine tasks) are extrapolated. Broader economy shows no major default cycle since 2008-2009, allowing bloat that AI could disrupt.
2. Hypothetical: Productivity rises at rates not seen since the 1950s, leading to "Ghost GDP" where output grows but doesn't circulate in the human economy.
Real Basis: Current AI-driven productivity gains are scaled up, referencing historical 1950s U.S. productivity growth (e.g., peaks of 9.69% quarterly) as a benchmark for post-WWII booms, contrasted with slower modern rates (e.g., 1.16% YoY in Q3 2025).
Sources: Historical 1950s data shows high growth, e.g., 9.69% in Dec 1950 and averages of 2.5% annually from 1948-1979. Current U.S. labor productivity growth is lower at 1.16% YoY in Sep 2025 .
3. Hypothetical: The human-centric consumer economy (70% of GDP) withers as AI displaces workers and velocity of money flatlines, since machines don't spend.
Real Basis: U.S. personal consumption is currently ~68-70% of GDP, forming the bedrock of economic circulation; the projection assumes AI reduces human wages/spending without replacing demand.
Sources: Personal consumption at 67.9% in Q4 2025 , 68.4% in Dec 2025 , 68.2% in Dec 2025 , and 67.93% in 2024 .
4. Hypothetical: White-collar income impairment exposes $13 trillion in mortgage risks, leading to reassessments of prime mortgages.
Real Basis: Current U.S. mortgage debt is ~$13 trillion, supporting the housing market; projection ties this to wage collapses from AI job losses.
Sources: Total mortgage debt at $13.17 trillion end of Dec 2025; grew by $524 billion in 2025 .
5. Hypothetical: Private credit defaults spike in mid-2027 due to AI disruption in software, after 17 years without a real default cycle.
Real Basis: No major U.S. default cycle since the 2007-2009 Great Recession (17 years by 2025-2026), leading to bloated private equity in software; AI is projected to trigger the next wave.
Sources: Last major recession/default cycle was 2007-2009 Great Recession; post-1945 recessions average 10 months, but none as severe recently .
6. Hypothetical: Agentic coding tools enable replication of mid-market SaaS products in weeks by late 2025, leading to pricing collapses and layoffs (e.g., ServiceNow's hypothetical Q3 2026 report showing 14% growth down from 23%, 15% workforce cut).
Real Basis: Tools like Claude Code (Anthropic) and Codex (OpenAI) exist in 2025-2026, allowing developers to accelerate coding; ServiceNow's current strong growth (21% YoY in Q4 2025) is projected to reverse as AI commoditizes software.
Sources: Claude Code as an agentic tool for codebases; OpenAI Codex as a full coding agent. ServiceNow Q4 2025: $3,466M subscription revenue, 21% growth; full-year 2025 $12.89B.
7. Hypothetical: By early 2027, LLM usage becomes default, with agents handling consumer decisions; Qwen’s open-source agentic shopper catalyzes this, leading to 400,000 tokens per person daily by March 2027 (10x from end-2026).
Real Basis: Qwen models (Alibaba) support agentic features in 2026; current global token usage is massive (e.g., OpenRouter >1T/day, OpenAI 8.6T/day in Oct 2025), projected to scale with agent adoption.
Sources: Qwen3.5 supports agentic capabilities and open-source agents [web:16, web:17, web:19]. Token usage: OpenRouter 100T+ tokens studied in 2025, >1T/day by early 2026; OpenAI 8.6T/day average ; no exact per-person average, but inference costs highlight scaling trends.
8. Hypothetical: Intermediation businesses collapse as agents remove friction in subscriptions, travel, insurance, financial advice, tax prep, legal work, and real estate (e.g., buy-side commissions compress from 2.5-3% to under 1% by 2027).
Real Basis: Current real estate commissions are 2.5-3% per agent (total 5-6%); agents are projected to eliminate inertia-based models across sectors.
Sources: Average buyer's agent commission 2.75-2.82% in 2025-2026, total ~5.57-5.70% [web:41, web:42]; downward trend from 3% in 1990s to 2.7% today ; standard split 2.5-3% .
9. Hypothetical: DoorDash's market fragments overnight in 2027 as coding agents enable dozens of competitors, with multi-app dashboards eroding incumbents.
Real Basis: DoorDash holds strong U.S. market share (55-65%) in 2026, but faces competitors like Uber Eats (23-35%) and Grubhub (16-20%); projection assumes AI lowers barriers to entry.
Sources: DoorDash 28.2% global marketplace share, Uber Eats 35.75%, Grubhub 19.67% ; U.S.-specific: 56-65% for DoorDash.
